Dalgona Coffee

ABOUT:

Dalgona coffee, also known as hand beaten coffee, is a beverage originating from Macau made by whipping equal parts instant coffee powder, sugar, and hot water until it becomes creamy and then adding it to cold or hot milk. Occasionally, it is topped with coffee powder, cocoa, crumbled biscuits, or honey. It was popularized on social media during the COVID-19 pandemic, when people refraining from going out started making videos of whipping the coffee at home, by hand without using electrical mixers. After the drink spread to South Korea, it was renamed "dalgona coffee" which is derived from dalgona, a Korean sugar candy, due to the resemblance in taste and appearance, though most dalgona coffee does not actually contain dalgona.


INGREDIENTS:

For Whipping

  • 0.5 tablespoon instant coffee
  • 1.5 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoons hot boiling water

Other Ingredients

  • 0.75 cups milk (at room temperature or chilled)
  • ice cubes (as required, optional)

For Topping

  • 0.13 teaspoon cocoa powder (optional)

INSTRUCTIONS:

Whipping Coffee Mixture

  1. Take coffee, sugar and hot boiling water in a mixing bowl.
  2. Using a small whisk begin to whip and beat till the mixture is creamy and thick.
  3. Use two ways of beating the coffee. First beat clockwise and then use vertical zig-zag motions. Alternate between the two. If the bowl is small, then slightly tilt the bowl and beat.
  4. To whip, can also use a small blender or mixer-grinder or a hand-held mixer (which is used for whipping cream or making cake batter).

Making Dalgona Coffee

  1. Add 2 to 3 ice cubes in a mug or glass.
  2. Divide 1.5 cups of milk between both the glasses.
  3. Now divide the whipped coffee equally in the two glasses.
  4. Sprinkle some cocoa powder on the coffee (optional). Serve.

NOTES

  • Use instant coffee only.
  • To make hot whipped coffee, swap the cold milk with hot milk.
  • You can add more sugar if you prefer.
